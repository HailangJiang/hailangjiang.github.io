About me

My name is Hailang Jiang [ˈhaɪ.lɑŋ d͡ʒɑŋ][1] (Mandarin: 江 海浪 [t͡ɕʲɑ́ŋ xài.lɑ̂ŋ]). Hailang means ‘sea wave.’

  1. Note that *[haɪ.læŋ d͡ʒæŋ], as indicated by the spelling of ang in my name, sounds strange to me which is more like ‘Hailan Jian’, because Mandarin speakers primarily use vowel backness as the main cue to distinguish between an [an]/[æn] and ang [ɑŋ], rather than the coda.

I grew up in Chongqing, a city in southwestern China. I moved to Beijing to study economics at Peking University, where I developed an interest in linguistics that eventually outweighed my interest in microeconomics and game theory. Then I spent two years in England, for which I feel a deep sense of hiraeth. At UCL, I enjoyed studying and researching with the three amazing phonologists there.

When I’m not doing linguistics, my regular interests include photography (cityscapes, nature, and portraits), hiking, playing Nintendo games, watching TV series, cooking, reading, and writing.

Gunstock, New Hampshire, 2024
