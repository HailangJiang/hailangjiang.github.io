About me

My name is Hailang Jiang [ˈhaɪ.lɑŋ d͡ʒɑŋ][1] (Mandarin: 江 海浪 [t͡ɕʲɑ́ŋ xài.lɑ̂ŋ]). Hailang means ‘sea wave’ in Chinese[2]. You are free to call me Hailang, or ‘Lang-lang’ the reduplicative form[3], just like my friends.

I grew up in Chongqing, a city in southwestern China. I received my undergraduate education at Peking University in Beijing, majoring in economics. Before phonology, I once had an interest in microeconomics and game theory which formally model human behaviors.

Now I am studying in the Master program in Linguistics (with a Specialization in Phonology) offered by UCL in London, UK, and I enjoy studying and researching with the phonologists there, Dr Andrew Lamont, Dr James White & Prof Andrew Nevins.

During my leisurely moments, I am ‘p-side’ as well; p for photography (of cityscapes, nature and people) and papercraft (namely, paper modeling, quilling, folding, and so on). I also enjoy hiking and traveling when I don’t have to work to a deadline. On a typical holiday, you’ll find me at home, diving into Nintendo Switch games, binge-watching TV series, preparing meals, or burning the midnight oil jotting down thoughts in my diary or crafting prose.


  1. Note that *[haɪ.læŋ d͡ʒæŋ], as indicated by the spelling of ang in my name, sounds strange to me which is more like ‘Hailan Jian’, because Mandarin speakers primarily use vowel backness as the main cue to distinguish between an [an]/[æn] and ang [ɑŋ], rather than the coda.
  2. And Jiang means ‘river’; you may find these three characters all contain the radical ‘氵’ three drops of water, which always indicates the meaning of water.
  3. In my native dialect of Chongqing Mandarin, it is common to use a fully reduplicated form of certain syllables that appear as the last syllable in one’s given name as a nickname.
